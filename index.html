Omni-channel experiences for brands, services and products

Future isn't doomed, though still scary.

The impact of new media, technologies and information systems brought new ways of dealing with design, aesthetics and multi-sensorial experiences. More than scrolling and clicking, our future aspires to be more and more intangible, made of ubiquitous interactive and reactive environments — immersive spaces that generate augmented perceptions. Virtual Reality and Artificial Intelligence are already allowing hyper performances, and if mostly our brains have been burning with life's development, soon our bodies and souls will unconsciously command the next world's most complex matrixes. Every heart beat will be tracked, as well as your thoughts, your moves, your sighs... and probably your farts!

But life can feel easier, better and delightful,
and I'm keen on supporting that.

My motivation to understand new media and technologies is driven by its constant quest to overcome itself, how it reflects and engages with several different areas (from sciences to art), and how it, most importantly, changes people's life. Crossing the boundaries of a creative designer I keep studying and researching about humans life experience, emotional intelligence, behavioural patterns and new interactions. Design thinking supports me achieving faster, creative and smarter solutions for products, services, and experiences in this world where designing for screens is no longer enough.

Cross-disciplinar, the creatives of tomorrow will design more than physical interfaces.

It's a vicious circle: the technological breakthrough allows the creatives and engineers to develop improved solutions for human needs, while at the same time, the more they push it, the more it generates "new" human needs. Will there be a limit?

Living a life balance in full power is what gives me the most motivation.

I come from Portugal, where I earned two Masters: New Media and Art & Multimedia.
After an internship as a Multimedia Designer and Researcher at YDreams (a company focused on augmented interactive installations, environmental sensing and mobile computing), a Leonardo da Vinci's scholarship took me to Switzerland where I joined Process AG, a branding and design consultancy.

In 2013 I started working at Liip AG – Digital Progress, following Holacracy's principles (a system for self-organization). Agile workflows and Scrum methodologies are the key for a better performance in crossfunctional teams. I directed art and experience design of Freitag omni-channel Brand Experience (awarded 7x), the opendata.swiss Government portal (awarded 3x), and the HWZ University of Applied Sciences website. I also worked with clients like: Helvetia, Credit Suisse, Swiss Life, Edelweiss Air, local.ch, Phonak, Nebis, Badewelten, Altium Capital.

In 2017 I became melt's Experience Director, and in 2018 I co-founded –naut with 2 friends, where we conceived interactive installations that connect the physical reality with the digital world, experiences that intersect art, design, technology, scenography and emerging digital practices.
